# About Nihar

## The short version

> "I never fit neatly into one discipline. Every time I thought I found 'my thing,' it opened the door to a larger system behind it."

Nihar Bhagat is a designer who started in **graphic design** and "somehow ended up deep in **developer tooling and infrastructure systems**." He's designed **brands, cultures, and products** — and what connected all of it was **systems thinking**.

His positioning line, used as the site title: **"Advanced Designer, Basic Conversationalist."**

## The trajectory

The work spans the full distance from abstraction to application. By his own framing the disciplines stack roughly like this:

- **UI and interaction design** — ~2 years
- **Studio-building and creative direction** — ~3 years
- **Developer tooling, tech infrastructure, and growth experiments** — ~3 years

The arc he tells about himself: *"Went pretty far behind the curtain. Started with interfaces. Ended up deep in systems work."* And: *"Got into design because of curiosity and stayed because I like making complicated things feel clear."*

## How he describes his practice

> "My practice has evolved from designing symbols to designing dashboards. I've found joy in breaking and making processes within live systems to make them flowww."

> "I live well to be able to design well. I want to make my life and lives of those around me beautiful."

> "Small interventions. In work. In daily life. And adjustments that make things function a bit better and make life feel a bit better."

> "Welcome to my collection of design interventions aka my portfolio."

The recurring frame: **design as a series of small, deliberate interventions in live systems** — and a refusal to separate living well from working well.
