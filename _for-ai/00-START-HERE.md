# Nihar Bhagat — Portfolio Brief

*A self-contained read of the portfolio at [nihar.works](https://nihar.works), written for an AI collaborator. Drop this folder into a Claude (or any LLM) Project and you have everything needed to brainstorm, critique, or write alongside Nihar — without his codebase.*

---

## Who Nihar is, in two paragraphs

Nihar Bhagat is a designer whose work runs the full ladder from **abstraction to application** — he started in graphic design (brand marks, identity, visual systems) and ended up deep in **developer tooling and infrastructure systems**, product design, and studio-building. The connective tissue across all of it is **systems thinking**: he treats design as *organized attention*, and is happiest "breaking and making processes within live systems to make them flow."

His self-description sets the tone: *"Advanced Designer, Basic Conversationalist."* Precise, a little wry, allergic to hype. He frames his portfolio not as a display case but as a **collection of design interventions** — small, deliberate changes "in work, in daily life" that make things "function a bit better and feel a bit better."

## What the portfolio is

A long-form **editorial** portfolio built as a set of **"sheet-stack reading environments"** — each project is an authored reading experience, not a scrollable gallery. The governing visual metaphor is a **flatlay**: objects meticulously arranged on a clean surface, each placed with intention. Everything "glides, settles, and lands" — paper-physical motion, never snappy or bouncy. The site wants to be *read*, not scrolled past.
