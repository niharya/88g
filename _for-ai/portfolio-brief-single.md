## Who Nihar is

A designer who runs the full ladder from **abstraction to application** — started in **graphic design** (brand marks, identity), ended up "deep in **developer tooling and infrastructure systems**," with product design and studio-building in between. The connective tissue is **systems thinking**. His own line: *"Advanced Designer, Basic Conversationalist."*

Trajectory (by his own framing): UI & interaction design (~2y) → studio-building & creative direction (~3y) → developer tooling, tech infrastructure & growth experiments (~3y). *"Went pretty far behind the curtain. Started with interfaces. Ended up deep in systems work."*

He frames the work as **a collection of design interventions** — *"Small interventions. In work. In daily life. And adjustments that make things function a bit better and make life feel a bit better."*

**Contact:** commissions@niharbhagat.com · linkedin.com/in/niharbhagat · github.com/niharya · x.com/neonihar
**Audience:** studio heads, creative directors, product leaders — people who read for craft.

## What the portfolio is

A long-form **editorial** portfolio built as **"sheet-stack reading environments."** Each project is an authored reading experience, not a scrollable gallery. The governing image is a **flatlay** — objects meticulously placed on a clean surface, each slightly off-true with a soft shadow. Core belief: **design is organized attention.** Tone: precise, calm, human — no hype.

Motion is **paper-physical**: everything glides, settles, lands. No bounce, no snap.

**Structural vocabulary:** *workbench* (the desk), *mat* (graph-paper surface), *sheet* (a chapter-scale mat with chrome), *surface* (a content panel on a mat), *rail* (a tucked note drawer), *marker* (docked nav tab). The throughline: elements feel **docked, tucked, or suspended with intention — never just placed nearby**, and **proof artifacts stay proof artifacts** (real sketches, never placeholder).

## Voice & taste (read before writing in his voice)

**Precise · calm · human.** Concrete over abstract; restraint over volume; warm and lightly wry. Tells: em-dashes and short fragments ("Small interventions. In work. In daily life."), first-person direct address, dry self-aware humor, one deliberate playful stretch now and then ("make them flowww"). Metaphors come from craft and systems — paper, surfaces, rhythm, flow — never tech-marketing.

**Anti-voice:** hype words ("seamless," "revolutionary," "passionate"), generic any-designer abstraction, recruiter-speak, over-cleaning his intentional quirks.

**Design principles:** reading-first not scroll-first; the flatlay grammar; docked/tucked/suspended relationships; controls must not lie; paper-physical motion; small interventions in live systems.

**References (his sourced taste):** Fraunces + Google Sans for type; OKLCH + Munsell for color; Atlassian elevation + Radix shadow-restraint; Framer Motion + Web Animations API; a slowed *Balatro* shader under `/rr`; editorial print lineage over web templates. Tools by job: **ChatGPT for thinking**, **Claude Code for building**, Figma for layout, v0 for the `/rr` game prototype.

**Collaborating with him:** he's a designer, not a CTO — treat asks as design conversations ("could we?" means "is this right?"), diagnose before fixing, recommend don't hedge, default to the smallest correct change.

## The work — route by route

**`/` (landing)** — Hero, an about section that expands short→long, a color *spectrum* running from Abstraction to Application, and a contact form sorted by intent (Hiring / Collaboration / Curiosity / Something else). Hero: *"I'm Nihar. I've designed brands, cultures, and products. What connected all of it was systems thinking."* Practice: *"My practice has evolved from designing symbols to designing dashboards… breaking and making processes within live systems to make them flowww."*

**`/all` — the Works hub** — An engraved "invitation" essay that opens into two browse modes: a **Longform** career timeline (case studies) and a **Visual** showcase grid. The index to the body of work (Connektion, Aleyr, Ecochain, Codezeros, Slangbusters, and the deep-dive cases). OG: *"Different systems. Same instincts." / "Trying to organize complexity so people can move through it and get somewhere."*

**`/biconomy` — the breadth project** — Two years as Product Designer at a blockchain payments infrastructure company, told in six chapters: a formal **UX Audit** (before/after slider across six dashboard flows), **Demos** (prototypes making the invisible SDK tangible), **BIPs** (a personal idea-list that became "Biconomy Improvement Proposals" in Notion), **Multiverse** (diagnosing team misalignment as "The Multiverse Theory," rendered as a poster), **API concepts** ("Science Fiction Meets Interface Fiction," pitched as Twitter threads), and **Staying Anchored** (turnover, self-documentation, and the relationships that kept the work coherent — closing on team photos). Theme: *making the invisible legible.* Tone: precise but warm, dry humor ("5 PMs, 3 FE devs, 2 BE devs, 1 bear cycle, 1 bull cycle").

**`/rr` — Rhythm & Reprogramming** — The most interactive route: a case study on designing **Rug Rumble**, a fast, memetic web3 strategy card game — told through a reading environment you can actually *play inside* (a playable game board, an animated shader rug, a Constraints scorecard, stacked cards). OG: *"I think games are just systems people agree to take seriously."* The playful, operate-don't-just-read project.

**`/marks` — graphic-design roots** — A cinematic, self-playing reel of six logo marks designed 2019–2024, shown as one continuous editorial document rather than a case study. Title set huge in Fraunces on near-black. *"Trying to reduce things until they still feel alive."* / *"Identity work before systems happened."*

**`/shape-of-product` — musings** — A calm, first-person writing layer on how he works — *"You are building tools for people who build tools."* / *"Most of my work has been in systems where design sits close to infrastructure."* The one place where new authoring is ongoing. Role: *"Make the system legible / So decisions can be made without guesswork."*

## The through-line

Nihar's work is **small, deliberate interventions in live systems** — graphic, product, and infrastructural — unified by systems thinking and an editorial, reading-first sensibility. When you brainstorm with him: specific over grand, calm over loud, intervention over reinvention.
